Introduction

Cyber-attacks have made a lot of losses to our society, some are from companies and others are from individuals.   An employee must know how to manage and mitigate an attack.

Learning objectives:

  • Social engineering education
  • Measures to manage cyber attacks
  • Protect my Device

Topic 1: Social engineering education

Employees should understand what is social engineering and educate themselves about the steps that can be used to trick them.

  • Staff should be well known about security responsibilities and should report any unusual activity such as phishing attacks.
  • Staff should learn the physicological triggers that can be used by SEs. SEs could create situations and sometimes confuse victims.
  • Staff should not open suspicious emails or click on links that are not required or trustworthy.
  • Never share a password or type in your password in an unprotected place.
  • Beware of clickjacking, never click on staff that is not required or suspicious.

 

 

Topic 2: Measures to manage Cyber Attacks

  • Update software immediately: updating can ensure the integrity of the content.
  • Maintain Privileges and Accounts: this involves higher administrative to manage and give fewer privileges to others.
  • System backup is important, employees should be responsible to have a proven backup strategy, which can help in real incidents.
  • Implement a multi-Authentication: This helps in managing cyber security risk.

 

Topic 3: Protect my Device

Protecting your device is also important:

  • Always use the most up-to-date firewalls, anti-malware, whitelisting, and spam filters to keep away malicious activities.
  • Use a strong unique password and never share it.
